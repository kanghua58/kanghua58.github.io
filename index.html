Hi, This is Kay!

I am a polyglot fluent in Chinese, English, Japanese, and Korean and currently a master's student learning Educational Technology at Human-Computer Interaction Institute at Carnegie Mellon University.

I came from a background in foreign language and literature and worked as a part-time language teacher teaching English, Chinese, and Korean to learners across all age groups.

Limited success in most language learning experiences can also be attributed to problems in learning material, execution, and feedback. However, with technology, it would be possible to assess learners’ current skills via self-adaptive placement tests, align them with texts and exercise questions at an appropriate level, and give them timely feedback on diction and grammar after each output. By driving the learning process based on learners’ data, we can make learning highly relevant to learners’ interests and tailored to their current levels and expected goals. At CMU, I dive into the theories behind and the approaches to data-informed learning.

I am eager to collaborate with organizations and individuals who share my values. If you're exploring potential partnerships, please get in touch!
